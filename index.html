Iconic trucks in the media

Farmers all over the world love them. No muddy terrain can stop or slow down them. no matter how, or where or when, they do what they have been designed for: offer you the best experience you will ever have. And the hundreds of horses under the hood are neighing and calling you for the wildest drive of your life, day after day. Every time they appear in a movie, they become the main hero. Let's see some old ads of some classy trucks from the past that continue to live in the present and most likely in the foreseeable future.

Quality wars

When it comes to quality, one name comes first. A company headquartered in Japan that started less than a century ago made its name a synonym of quality. Countless genrations of managers follow the simple principles discovered bay a chosen few employees of the aforementioned company. Its name ? Toyota, of course. One of the most reliable car they ever made is the Hilux, the pickup that goes on every type of terrain over and over again.

Industry strikes back

The company that invened the mass manufacturing could not stop itself from making a hell of a car. Some call it the farmer's car. Others just like its solidity or the power of its engine. The Veyron may have 100PS, but the F150 is not far behind. And it is one of the all-time bestsellers of the Ford Motor Company for good reason. Countles farmers rely on it to carry their goods to market. Heavyweight film stars drive it because it is the only car that fits with their stature. Last, but not least, the F150 is an all-terrain car that time and again proves a reliable choice for wild riders.

Return of the giant

The largest things man has ever built or carved needed the biggest tools available. When it comes to heavy machinery carrying dozens of tons, there are but a few choices and on of them is always standing out. The truck with the biggest tires, featuring the largest and loudest engine and driven from a small cabiin 30 feet up always takes the king's place. Liebherr's T264 is one of a kind. You might not be able to park it on your street because it is bigger than teh street itself, taller than your house and no one can stop it in a chase.
